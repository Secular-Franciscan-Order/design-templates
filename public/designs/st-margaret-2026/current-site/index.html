WELCOME TO THE ST MARGARET OF CORTONA FRATERNITY

We are Secular Franciscans living in the desert of Las Vegas, Nevada. We are so happy that you have found us. Our Fraternity is part of the Saint Thomas More Region which covers southern Nevada and Arizona.

Our Mission Statement: We live the gospel way of life, following in the footsteps of Francis and Clare, touching the world with our blessings, talents, and faith. We give from what we have to those who have less. We bring hope and love to the hopeless and unloved. We interact with the world with humility, charity and grace. We pray for the needs of our community and the world, while giving thanks and praise to God, following the guidelines set forth by our regional, national and international leadership. We live and teach by example. We follow our beloved Francis by preaching the Gospel at all times and when necessary, we use words. Our profession is a grace and gift of the Holy Spirit.

Who We Are

Is God Calling You to the Secular Franciscan Order?

To become a Secular Franciscan, you must be an actively practicing Catholic, of any of the rites in union with Rome.

The process of becoming a professed Secular Franciscan is a journey that involves three separate stages and culminates in a lifelong commitment to live the gospel following the example of St. Francis of Assisi. This formation process unfolds in regularly scheduled formation sessions during which the study material is thoroughly discussed.

Orientation

The first stage provides time for dialogue and developing relationships in fraternity. You will be introduced to the lives of St. Francis and St. Clare, share in Franciscan prayer life, and discern if the Spirit is calling you to a Secular Franciscan vocation. Orientation is a minimum of three months.

Inquiry

The first formal period of initiation is a time of in-depth study of St. Francis, St. Clare, the Franciscan charism, and Franciscan history. The period of Inquiry is a minimum of six months. If a vocation is discerned, the Inquirer is received into the Order.

Candidacy

The final formal period of initiation prepares you for permanent commitment by immersion into fraternity life. The period of Candidacy is a minimum of eighteen months and culminates in permanent commitment to the gospel life.

After profession of the Rule and permanent commitment to the gospel way of life, the newly professed member joins the rest of the fraternity in ongoing formation.

FAQ

Q: WHO ARE THE FRANCISCANS?

A: We Franciscans owe our inspiration to Francesco di Bernardone, an affluent young merchant from Assisi, who in 1206 renounced his wealth and social status in favor of a life dedicated to God and the least of God's people. The vision Francis had for his community was that the brothers would live together, pray together, support one another like a family, and work in the world among ordinary members of society.

Q: WHO IS ST. FRANCIS OF ASSISI?

A: St. Francis of Assisi founded the orders of the Friars Minor, the Poor Clares, and the lay Third Order. His devotion to the human Jesus, evangelical poverty, charity, and desire to follow Christ's example made him one of the most venerated figures in Roman Catholic history.

Q: WHO ARE/WHAT IS A SECULAR FRANCISCAN?

A: Secular Franciscans live in the world rather than in religious communities. They may be single or married, women or men, in all walks of life. They live the Gospel in a Franciscan manner according to their own Rule, professed after a period of initial formation.

Q: CAN ANYONE JOIN THE SECULAR FRANCISCAN ORDER?

A: You can be a Franciscan at heart without joining the Order. If you wish to take the step to join the Ordo Franciscanus Saecularis, you should be a practicing Catholic and over 18 years of age. You can be married or single, laity or clergy.

Q: WHAT IS EXPECTED OF ME IF I JOIN THE ORDER?

A: Members of the OFS are encouraged to live a Christ-like Gospel life through prayer, Mass, regular fraternity meetings, ongoing spiritual formation, and a heart turned toward the poor and toward peace.

Q: DO YOU LIVE LIKE MONKS AND NUNS?

A: No. Members of the OFS live at home and meet in fraternity, usually in local Church buildings. The OFS is a secular life for people who do not live in a convent, friary, priory, or abbey.
