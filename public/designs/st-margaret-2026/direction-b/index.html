Who We Are

Ordinary lay Catholics following Jesus in the joyful way of Francis and Clare.

We're not monks or nuns. We have jobs, families, and busy lives - and we've found that the Franciscan path makes all of it richer. We serve people who have less, care for creation, and bring a little more hope to our corner of Las Vegas.

Is this for me?

About the Franciscans
Who are the Franciscans?+

Followers of St. Francis of Assisi, who gave up wealth to live close to Jesus and the poor. His family today includes friars, sisters, and lay people - Secular Franciscans - living his Gospel spirit in the everyday world.

What does "Secular Franciscan" mean?+

"Secular" means we live in the world - in our homes, families, workplaces, and neighborhoods - rather than in a monastery. We are fully Franciscan and fully part of ordinary life.

Do you live like monks and nuns?+

Not at all. We take no monastic vows and we live in our own homes. We keep our jobs, marry, raise families, and bring the Franciscan spirit into ordinary daily life.

About joining
Can anyone join the Secular Franciscan Order?+

Any practicing Catholic - single or married, age 18 or up - who feels drawn to this way of life is warmly welcome to begin discerning with us.

How long does it take to join?+

The path usually begins with orientation, then inquiry, then candidacy. It unfolds over time so you can pray, ask questions, build friendships, and discern freely before profession.

What if I'm just curious and not ready to commit?+

Curiosity is a good beginning. You are welcome to visit a gathering, meet the fraternity, and ask questions with no pressure to continue.

What's expected of me
What is expected of me if I join?+

A life of daily prayer, regular Mass, faithful presence at our monthly gatherings, and a heart open to the poor and to peace - all lived out within your own everyday circumstances.

Do I have to wear a habit?+

No. Secular Franciscans usually wear a simple Tau cross as a sign of our profession and our connection to Saint Francis.
