Who We Are

We are lay Catholics living the Gospel in the footsteps of Francis and Clare — serving those who have less, and carrying hope into our city.

Read our full profession +

The Secular Franciscan Order is a community of Catholic men and women who seek to follow Christ in the way of Saint Francis of Assisi, while living in their own homes and families. We are not monks or nuns; we keep our jobs, raise our children, and pray amid ordinary life. Called to go "from gospel to life and life to gospel," we strive to make the love of Christ visible — through prayer, through fraternity, and through service to the poor, the lonely, and all of creation.

Is this for me?

Questions, gently answered

About the Franciscans
Who are the Franciscans?+

We are the spiritual family of St. Francis of Assisi (1181–1226), who left wealth behind to follow the poor and humble Christ. Today his family includes friars, sisters, and lay men and women — Secular Franciscans — who live his Gospel way of life in the world.

What does "Secular Franciscan" mean?+

"Secular" simply means we live in the everyday world — in our homes, families, and workplaces — rather than in a monastery. We are fully Franciscan, and fully part of ordinary life.

Do you live like monks and nuns?+

No. We are not monks or nuns and we take no monastic vows. We keep our jobs, marry, raise families, and pray within the rhythm of normal life.

About joining
Can anyone join the Secular Franciscan Order?+

Any practicing Catholic in good standing — single or married, age 18 or older — who feels drawn to the Franciscan way is welcome to begin discerning with us.

How long does it take to join?+

There is no rush. Formation moves through Orientation, Inquiry, and Candidacy over roughly three years before profession — time meant for genuine, unhurried discernment.

What if I'm just curious and not ready to commit?+

Then you are exactly who we hope to meet. Come to a gathering with no expectations. "Come and see" is how every Franciscan journey begins.

What's expected of me
What is expected of me if I join?+

A life of daily prayer, regular participation in the Mass, faithful presence at our monthly fraternity gatherings, and a heart turned toward the poor and toward peace — lived out in your own circumstances.

Do I have to wear a habit?+

No. Secular Franciscans wear ordinary clothes. Many wear a simple Tau cross as a quiet sign of their way of life.
